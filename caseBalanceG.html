CASE BRIEF

The project brief provided by Balance golf, co developed by Esbjörn Ebbesson, described the PROBLEM AREA LIKE THE FOLLOWING: The website is NOT STRUCTURED OR ADAPTED FOR THE TARGET GROUP. It is neither designed in such a way that it explains or captures our golfing concept as a whole. Initially the design team questioned this brief as the website as a whole seemed to be relatively well designed. By further examining the website with the project brief in mind the design team could uncover ISSUES IN THE INFORMATIONAL ARCHITECTURE OF THE WEBSITE. Therefore this became the focus of the project.

RESULTS

The project resulted in a highly detailed design manual provided to Balance Golf, the designmanual cosists of the following:

• A mixed fidelity prototypein Figma

• A Coded prototype

• A sitemap for increased ease of navigation and improved informational architecture

• An ER-model

• Guidelines for further development, included in the project report

1/5

TAKEAWAYS

With this being my first design project in a team I gained a lot of insight into the intricacies of working within a team. During the project I was responsible for evaluation, this meant that I planned and developed methods for generating data relating to the user experience (UX) of the artefact. I was also responsible for planning and developing the interview guides for the project.

I learned how hard it is to create interview guides from scratch, and how something that seems so simple can take a lot of time to get right. The same is true for creating methods of evaluation for the original artefact. As well as the improved iterations created by the project team. Therefore I garnished a lot of knowledge of how to use and modify existing evaluation criteria for artefacts and interview guide templates.

I also quickly came to the realisation that what the brief says is needed for a specific project, isn't always necessarily true. What the brief says the client needs is often colored with the bias of the client and as a design team each member has to take on the responsibility of examining the brief critically with an observing eye.

I came to the realisation that there's not always major differences in users' needs for information based on their age. But the needs, at least in this case, had granular differences. Older users wanted to have a deeper emotional connection with the trainers, whilst younger users didn't have this need and mostly wanted to know more about the practical aspects of the concepts.

With this being my first design project I gained a deeper understanding of how there's no perfect design solution. And no matter how long you spend on a design solution the design will never be perfect. Furthermore I understood how hard it is to design a solution when you're not in close contact with the client. But I also realised that you can't put the project on hold for too long even if it's hard to get a hold of your client because at the end of the day you still have to deliver a solution, whether or not the client was involved or not.

Finally, and probably my biggest takeaway, I learned a lot about how to gather and analyse data. Both how to continuously analyse and themathise interviewees answers during an interview or evaluation, as well as analysing and themathising large datasets from interviews. This is something that is quite time consuming and hard to be efficient at. But to me, it's very stimulating and very fun. I came to the realisation that i love analysing and seeing abstract patterns in data relating to users thoughts, feelings and actions toward certain design choices.

TAKEAWAYS - TL;DR

Interview guides - Designing interview guides are challenging. Using guidelines when learning to create interview guides can help a lot. Trying to imagine possible answers to each question asked in an interview can help further shape a iterate the questions.

Critically analysing the brief - Starting of by critically examining the clients own percevied wants and need in the brief is a good starting point. Sometimes clients arent really awware of what they actually need to solve a specific problem.

Differences in needs for users - Different demographic user groups, can seemingly have the same needs and wants. But you have to look carefullyy for granular differences in these needs and wants, because theyre almost always present.

No perfect design solution - Altough kind of obvious, theres never gonna be one, singular, perfect design solution that fits every specific member.

Gathering and analysing data - A seemingly monotonous task, that i find very stimulating. Theres something about the challenge of gathering data and then analysing it that just the perfect amount of challenging and fun!

RESEARCH

The design team began the project by examining the brief and looking over the artefact the brief was based on. The team could see issues with the original artefact and I started to plan and design an evaluation. I ended up basing the evaluation on Andy Budds “Heuristics For Modern Web Application Development”, and the project team performed the evaluation together.

After the evaluation was complete the project team concluded that the major issue that the artefact faced was a less than ideal informational and navigational architecture. The team kept these things in mind when moving forward. We concluded that we needed input from users regarding what information that was missing and what information that was superfluous.

Keeping the heuristic evaluation in mind I started to design a interview guide that would help the project team gain a deeper understanding of what potential users wanted to see on the website.

Andy Budds - Heuristics For Modern Web Application Development

Clarity - Make the system as clear, concise, and meaningful as possible for the intended audience

Minimize Unnecessary Complexity and Cognitive Load - Make the system as simple as possible for users to accomplish their tasks

Provide Users with Context - Interfaces should provide users with a sense of context in time and space

Promote a Pleasurable and Positive User Experience - The user should be treated with respect, and the design should be aesthetically pleasing and promote a pleasurable and rewarding experience

IDEATION

After the initial research was done the project team wanted to define a sitemap with improved navigational and informational architecture. To do this the team used cardsorting with potential users of the site, to gain a understanding of what, to them, was the most intuitive way to structure the website.

Next up the team created a persona based on interviews, this was done to further understand and empathize with what users need and wanted to see on the website.

After this a sitemap was drafted and itterated upon.

Finally all group members drafted sketches based on the final iteration of the sitemap. Each sketch had its own strength and weaknesses, therefore the group merged different placements of elements / functions of the different sketches into one! A Process i like calling "Frankensteining".

1/8

PROTOTYPING & EVALUATION

The prototype was created in Figma. And the project team could then move on to user testing. I developed the initial draft for the user testing which consisted of different navigation tasks that were timed, followed by questions aimed towards the users that were taking part in the usertesting.

By measuring the completion time of the navigation tasks clear outliers could be identified and the team could take measures to iterate the navigation even further to make it more effective. The followup questions were mainly geared towards gaining a deeper understanding of what the users thought about the navigation oh the website and how it could be improved further.

FINAL ARTEFACT

The project resulted in a design manual that was provided to Balance golf. The design manual was a highly detailed framework of how to improve the informational architecture of the Balance golf website in accordance with L., Morville, P., & Arango, J. (2015). Information architecture : for the Web and beyond (4. ed.). O’Reilly.

The designmanual cosists of things such as:

• A sitemap for increased ease of navigation and improved informational architecture

• A mixed fidelity prototype in Figma

• An ER-model

• A coded prototype

• And information about further development
