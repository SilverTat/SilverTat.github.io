CASE BRIEF

Due to mixups with initial brief the project group and the representative from Ambulanssjukvården Region Halland drafted a new brief. This was done due to another colluege group also recieving the same brief. The new project brief was designed around a different problem space that our group could work on.

The new briefs problem space encompassed Ambulandssjukvården Region halland and the bemanningsenheten. Today region hallands bemanningsenheten is in need of a more effective system for booking personnel who can drive and administer proper care inside of ambulances. This includes dividing work hours more efficiently and equally, seeing what competences different individuals have as well as having an efficient overview of work hours that needs to be filled and what competencies are needed inside the ambulance.

RESULTS

The project resulted in a booking system catered towards both the administrators and personnel of the system. The artefact aimed to streamline booking done by the administrators of the system as well as giving personnel a better overview, and more security in their work hours. With this said, a project report was also developed, but further improvements and evaluations of the artefact needs to be carried out before its put to use inside of such a sensitive area.

However the representatives were very happy with the prototype that was developed for them in such a short timespan. And that it gave them a lot of insights into how a possible booking system could work in the future.

RESEARCH

After establishing the brief and, with it the need for a proper booking system for Bemmaningsenheten. We started off with a competitor analysis, where we studied other booking systems used amongst other companies.

These booking systems were graded on four categories, that were deemed important. The four categories that were graded was, simpleness of use, ease of booking, cost of the booking medium and finally availability and flexibility for actors. The project team garnered insights regarding how the artefact should preferably function.

Interviews were also conducted with administrative and other personnel within Ambulandssjukvården Region Halland to garner insight into how the system works today. After this the project team used cooperative design methods to help lay the groundwork for a sitemap; personas were also developed together with the interviewees.

Some of the most important insights garnered was that the booking system should be easy to learn, should be limited to a specific app where all the information is stored, this to avoid notifications being lost amidst other notifications e.g. amongst users private mails.

GRADED CATEGORIES - EXPLANATION

Simpleness of use - How easy is the artefact to get used to and understand?

Ease of booking different timeslots - How does the solution make sure booking timeslots is efficient and user friendly?

Cost of the booking medium - Is the booking medium competitivley priced for the experience its offering?

Availability and flexibility for actors - Is the availability and flexibility of the solution satisfactory for actors within ambulanssjukvården - Region Halland?

IDEATION

The project team started of the ideation phase by co-designing personas with individuals from Region Halland who would use the system. This was done at the end of each conducted interview.

With the persona as a base, a scenario could be developed to further increase understanding, and empathize with the users.

Next up the project team started creating a sitemap prototype based on the analysed interviews.

Once the sitemap was completed each team member created different wireframes these were created on paper; and in the wireframing tool balsamiq. These different wireframes and their elements were discussed and Frankensteined together based on their strengths and weaknesses.

PROTOTYPING

A high fidelity prototype based on the final frankensteined wireframe was then created in figma. This prototype served to show the navigational architecture, and a booking system in high fidelity. And was ultimately meant as a highly detailed example of how a future implementation would look and work in practice.

Besides the figma prototype a coded prototype was created by me; in react native. The prototype was of mixed fidelity. Initially I expected it'd be easy to create a calendar, but I ran into problems when trying to make it dynamic. A variety of different prebuilt calendars were tested before settling on an adequate one. The coded prototype was created solely for the purpose of showing that it was technically possible to create a booking system, and was far from as complete as the figma prototype, and only a proof of concept.

1/2

EVALUATION

The project team developed an evaluation guide for the Figma prototype, focusing on task completion time and follow-up questions about the prototype's navigational and informational architecture. Four evaluations were conducted: two with non-expert users and two with expert users of the current system. These interviews yielded minor insights into improvements, such as clarifying how to display the selected day and the current day. However, the limited sample size likely restricted the amount of feedback gathered.

Most major issues were addressed in the early development stages through critique sessions with other students during the design studio. These sessions highlighted areas for improvement, particularly in presenting information more clearly, such as specifying the region a shift pertained to and enhancing button readability.

The evaluation process led to significant refinements in the final artefact, including a streamlined menu structure, a clearer schedule layout, larger buttons for easier interaction, and updated schedule markers. For instance, marking the current date was a topic of extensive debate, influenced by variations in how other scheduling applications handle this feature. Ultimately, the team settled on a marking approach that balanced functionality and user preferences.
